Aberrant activity of CDKs is linked to cancer, making them attractive targets for therapeutic ligands. This post explores the science of ligand binding to CDKs and its implications in drug discovery. --- ## What are CDKs? CDKs are serine/threonine kinases that regulate cell cycle transitions. They require binding to cyclins for activation. Common CDKs include: | CDK Type | Function | Cyclin Partner | |----------|----------|----------------| | CDK1 | Entry into mitosis | Cyclin B | | CDK2 | DNA synthesis | Cyclin A/E | | CDK4/6 | G1 phase progression | Cyclin D | {: .box-note} **Note:** CDK inhibitors are a cornerstone of cancer treatment, with CDK4/6 inhibitors like *Palbociclib* being FDA-approved for breast cancer therapy. --- ## Ligand Binding: Mechanisms CDK inhibitors work by blocking ATP or substrate binding. There are two primary mechanisms of ligand binding: 1. **ATP-Competitive Inhibitors**: These ligands bind to the ATP pocket, directly inhibiting kinase activity. ![ATP-Competitive Inhibition](https://via.placeholder.com/800x400?text=Ligand+Binding+Pocket){: .mx-auto.d-block :} 2. **Allosteric Inhibitors**: These ligands bind outside the active site, altering the protein conformation. --- ## Notable CDK Inhibitors Here's a quick overview of some widely studied CDK inhibitors: | Inhibitor | Target CDKs | Therapeutic Use | |----------------|-------------|---------------------------| | **Palbociclib** | CDK4/6 | Breast Cancer | | **Ribociclib** | CDK4/6 | Hormone Receptor-Positive Cancer | | **Flavopiridol** | Pan-CDK | Leukemia | --- ## Visualizing Ligand Docking Molecular docking simulations provide insights into ligand binding.
